Student Learns HTML

Once upon a time, there was a student who decided to learn HTML. At first, they thought it was easy as pie. But soon they realized that HTML was trickier than a fox in a henhouse. The student spent hours trying to figure out why their code was broken, only to find out that they had misspelled a tag. It was as if the HTML gods were playing a prank on them. But the student persisted, like a warrior fighting a dragon. They learned to conquer HTML, and even started to think in tags. Now, they're a coding ninja, ready to take on the world one web page at a time.

One day, the student proudly showed their newly acquired HTML skills to a friend. But the friend just scoffed and said, "That's so 90s. Nowadays, you need to learn React to be cool." The student's heart sank like a stone. Had they wasted all that time learning an outdated technology? But then, they had a moment of clarity. HTML was like an old, reliable car that may not have all the bells and whistles of a fancy new model, but still gets you where you need to go. And besides, who wants to be cool when you can be classic? The student smiled, knowing that they had learned something valuable and timeless.
